How-to Guide

Getting started with
Parametric Lab

Parametric Lab generates wreath structure shapes from mood and style choices — not from drawing. This guide walks through every panel in the app, what each control does, and how to get from a first shape to an exported blueprint.

7 steps Craft tier ~10 min read No drawing required
1

What Parametric Lab does

Understanding the tool before you start

Parametric Lab is a shape generator. You pick a shape family, dial in how it should feel, and the tool draws the structure for you. It doesn't place flowers — that's Layout Studio's job. Parametric Lab handles the shape underneath everything else.

Think of it as the frame-maker. A round wreath that's dense and formal looks very different from one that's airy and loose. Parametric Lab makes sure the structure matches the design intent before you spend time placing florals on the wrong foundation.

◎
When to use it
Use Parametric Lab at the start of any project where the shape isn't standard. If a client asks for something crescent-shaped, asymmetric, or trailing, start here. If they want a basic round wreath, you can skip straight to the Blueprint Engine or Layout Studio.
Evercrafted · Parametric Lab Craft

The app is divided into three work zones: the Shape Family panel at the top, the Mood Parameters sliders in the middle, and the Shape Preview canvas below. Work top to bottom. The AI Interpretation and Export panels sit below the canvas as optional final steps.

Choosing a shape family

Five forms, each with its own structural logic

The Shape Family panel shows five shape options. Click one to select it — the canvas updates immediately. The selected shape is highlighted in green with a subtle glow border.

Round
The default. Adjustable from tight and formal to loose and organic.
Crescent
Elegant and directional. Great for doorways and arched spaces.
Trailing
A ring with a flowing tail. Dramatic and naturally asymmetric.
Loose
Irregular and organic. Looks gathered rather than framed.
Figure 8
Two linked rings. Layered display or dual-season builds.
◌
How to choose
If the client hasn't specified a shape, start with Round as your baseline. Try Crescent for formal entryways, Trailing when they want something more dramatic or sculptural, Loose for garden-party or cottagecore aesthetics, and Figure 8 for large statement installations or layered displays.
3

Setting the mood parameters

Four sliders that drive all proportions

The Mood Parameters panel has four sliders. Each one runs 1–10 and shows its current value in green on the right. Drag any slider and the canvas updates immediately — there's no Apply button.

Density
1 = Airy  ·  10 = Lush
Controls how thick and full the shape reads. A high density adds more visual weight to the stroke and increases shadow depth. A low density creates a lighter, more minimal ring. This is the single biggest influence on how a design feels before any florals are added.
7
Structure
1 = Relaxed  ·  10 = Formal
Controls how precise and even the shape feels. A high structure value makes the shape tighter and more geometric — good for formal or minimal designs. A low value makes the shape softer and more garden-like. Also affects the canvas grid guide's tightness.
5
Scale
1 = Intimate  ·  10 = Statement
Controls the overall size of the drawn shape on the canvas. A low value keeps the shape compact — useful for a smaller wreath or a tight base ring. A high value pushes the shape outward for a larger, more commanding structure. This translates to physical size when you export to Layout Studio.
5
Asymmetry
1 = Symmetrical  ·  10 = Organic
Adds subtle irregularity and jitter to the shape path. At 1, the shape is perfectly even. As you increase it, the path develops natural variation — useful for designs that should look hand-foraged rather than manufactured. Works especially well on Loose and Trailing shapes. On Round, it softens the ring slightly.
3
◎
Tip — client vocabulary translation
When a client says "lush and full" → Density 7–9. "Light and airy" → Density 2–4. "Clean and modern" → Structure 8–10, Asymmetry 1–2. "Natural and gathered" → Structure 3–5, Asymmetry 6–8. "Statement piece" → Scale 8–10.
4

Reading the shape preview

What the canvas is showing you

The Shape Preview canvas draws your shape in real time. It's not a finished render — it's a structural guide showing the ring, stroke weight, and proportional layout before any florals are added.

Three things are always visible on the canvas:

  1. The structure rings — faint dashed circles showing the inner, mid, and outer placement zones. These guide where focal, supporting, and filler elements will sit when you move to Layout Studio.
  2. The shape path — the green stroke is your wreath ring. Its weight represents the Density value. Its regularity (or lack of it) represents Asymmetry.
  3. The metadata strip — the label at the top-left reads the current size, shape, density, and structure at a glance (e.g. 24″ · Crescent · Density 7 · Structure 5). The shape name appears bottom-right.
◌
Regenerate button
The ↺ Regenerate button redraws the shape with a new random jitter seed — useful on Loose and Trailing shapes where the organic variation matters. Hit it a few times until the irregular path looks right.
5

Using AI interpretation

Turn a description into a full blueprint spec

The AI Blueprint Interpretation panel is optional but powerful. Type a plain-language description of your shape idea and the engine returns a structured design brief — element counts, emotional tone, palette direction, and quality scores.

How to use it:

  1. Type a shape description in the input field. It doesn't have to be technical — plain language works best. Example: "crescent shape, airy and minimal, for a spring entryway"
  2. Click Interpret →. The button grays out while the engine runs (usually 3–6 seconds).
  3. The status line below the button shows progress, then confirms the blueprint ID when complete.
  4. The result card expands below with the full interpretation.

◐
If the engine can't connect
The status line will say "Could not reach the engine." This usually means a network issue. Wait a moment and try again — the input field keeps your description so you don't have to retype it.

Exporting your shape

Three ways to take the shape forward

The Export & Handoff panel offers three options. Two are available on Craft tier, one requires Studio.

SVG Export
Downloads the shape as a scaleable vector file. Use in any design tool — Illustrator, Procreate, Canva — as a placement guide or print template.
Craft+
→ Layout Studio
Sends the shape directly to Layout Studio with all parameters intact. Your Density, Structure, Scale, and Asymmetry values travel with the handoff so the canvas loads ready to use.
Craft+
Blueprint JSON
Full export with WGS genome string, zone placement data, and all blueprint metadata. Used for the marketplace and developer integrations.
Studio+
◎
Recommended workflow
For most projects, use → Layout Studio directly — it's the fastest path to placing florals. Use SVG Export when a client needs a physical template to print, or when you're handing off to someone who doesn't use Evercrafted. The Blueprint JSON export is for developers and marketplace sellers building on the platform.

Session vs. saved
Session History is in-memory and clears when you close the tab. To keep a shape permanently, click Save Shape on the canvas panel. Saved shapes appear in your Library in the left sidebar and persist across sessions.
