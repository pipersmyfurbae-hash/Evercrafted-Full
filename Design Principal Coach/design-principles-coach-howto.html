What this is

Your wreath design education — built into the platform.

Design Principles Coach is a live, AI-powered learning tool inside your Evercrafted Studio dashboard. It doesn't teach generic design theory — it teaches design in the language of wreath making: polar coordinates, stem counts, clock-position placement, and seasonal palettes. Think of it as having a master florist on call, anytime you're building.

1

Open the app from your Studio dashboard

Find Design Principles Coach listed under your Studio tier apps. Click to open — it loads instantly in your browser. No separate login, no downloads.

2

Choose a module to start with

You'll see four tabs at the top: Balance, Rhythm, Focal Points, and Color Theory. Each one is a self-contained learning module with a reference panel and a live AI coach. Start wherever feels most relevant to what you're building right now.

New to design? Start with Balance — it's the foundation everything else builds on.
3

Read the reference panel, then ask questions

The left side of the screen shows principle cards, diagrams, and placement examples for your active module. The right side is your AI chat coach. Use the reference panel to ground yourself, then ask the coach to apply those principles to your specific design.

The four modules

Each module is a discipline.
Each discipline is a superpower.

The modules aren't a curriculum you follow in order — they're tools you reach for when you need them. Feeling like a design is off? Open Balance. Not sure why your wreath feels static? Try Rhythm.

⚖️

Balance

Teaches symmetrical, asymmetrical, and radial balance with exact polar coordinates. Includes an interactive placement diagram for 24" wreaths showing where P (primary), S (secondary), and f (filler) clusters live.

The AI coach

Ask it anything. It speaks wreath.

The coach is scoped to whichever module you have open — so when you're in Balance, every answer is grounded in balance principles. Switch to Focal Points and it shifts completely. You never have to explain the context twice.

How the quick-prompt buttons work

Each module comes loaded with four quick-prompt buttons — these are the most common questions for that discipline. Tap any one to send it instantly. They're a great way to start a conversation or explore a principle you haven't thought about yet.

Example questions that work well

Balance "My 24-inch wreath feels heavy at the bottom. Walk me through how to redistribute the weight."
Balance "What's the exact polar coordinate for the primary focal on an asymmetric 18-inch layout?"
Rhythm "I have burgundy peonies and eucalyptus. How do I create an A-B alternation pattern?"
Rhythm "How many repetitions do I need before a pattern reads as intentional rather than random?"
Focal Points "I have two large blooms that are competing. How do I make one clearly dominant?"
Focal Points "Design me an eye path for a fall wreath with a primary focal at 315° and two secondaries."
Color Theory "Build me a 60-30-10 fall palette using burgundy as my dominant color with a warm accent."
Color Theory "My palette feels too warm and heavy. What cool-toned greenery should I add, and how many stems?"

Keep the conversation going

The coach remembers your full conversation within the session. If the first answer raises a follow-up question, just ask — it has the context. You don't need to re-explain your design every time. Start a new session (by switching modules or refreshing) to reset the context.

Design Score Analyzer

Describe your design.
See exactly what's working.

Scroll below the module area to find the Design Score Analyzer. Describe your current design in plain language — florals, placement, size, colors — and the coach will score it across all four principles. You'll get a number and a one-sentence repair note for anything that needs attention.

1

Write a plain-language design description

Don't worry about being technical. Describe what you have: the size, the main flowers, roughly where you placed things, and the color palette. The more specific you are, the sharper the score.

Good example: "24-inch grapevine, large burgundy peonies clustered near the top-left, eucalyptus filler spread around the ring, small amber berries as accents, mostly warm tones."
2

Hit "Score It" and read your results

Four score cards will appear — one for each principle. Each shows a score from 0–100, a color-coded bar, and a single repair note. Focus first on any axis scoring below 70.

3

Take the repair note back to the coach

Copy the repair note for your weakest score and paste it into the coach chat in the relevant module. Ask: "Help me fix this." The coach will walk you through specific changes.

Score ranges explained
80–100 — Strong. This principle is working well in your design.
60–79 — Decent, but improvable. Read the repair note for a targeted fix.
0–59 — Needs attention. Take the repair note directly to the coach for a walkthrough.
What each axis scores
Balance — Visual weight distribution across the ring
Rhythm — Pattern, repetition, and flow direction
Focal Impact — Clarity of primary focal and eye path
Color Harmony — Palette ratio, warm/cool balance, seasonal fit

Pro tips

Get more out of every session.

These habits will help you build faster, score higher, and actually internalize what you're learning — not just get through the exercise.

★

Score before you build, not just after

Describe your intended design before you start building and run a score. You'll catch structural problems — like a focal placement that will compete with your secondary — before you've already wired everything in.

★

Use the module you struggle with most, not the one you like

Most makers have one or two principles they naturally get and one or two they skip. The coach works best when you lean into the uncomfortable ones. If your color is always decent but your rhythm is always flat, live in the Rhythm module for a while.

★

Give the coach a specific design, not a general question

"How does rhythm work?" will get you a solid answer. "I have a 20-inch wreath with 5 white hydrangeas and lamb's ear — how do I create rhythm?" will get you an actionable plan with stem counts and intervals. Specificity is the multiplier.

★

Run the same design through all four modules

A design that scores 90 on balance can still score 55 on rhythm. Use all four axes to build a complete picture of your design's strengths and gaps. The best designs score 75+ across the board.

Quick reference

Do's and Don'ts.

A fast reference for getting the best results from the coach and the scorer.

Action Why it matters
DoInclude your wreath size when asking questionsStem counts and placement intervals are size-specific. "18-inch" and "24-inch" get very different answers.
DoName the specific florals you're working withThe coach references real species — "burgundy peonies" gets better advice than "large red flowers."
DoFollow up if an answer feels offThe coach has your conversation history. Push back, ask for clarification, or ask it to explain differently.
DoUse the quick-prompt buttons as jumping-off pointsThey're designed to surface the most useful questions for each module — great for when you're not sure what to ask.
Don'tAsk about cherry blossoms, pussy willow, or twig-based blossom floralsThese are permanently excluded from Evercrafted designs by platform guidelines.
Don'tRun a score with a vague description"I made a wreath with flowers and greenery" won't produce a useful score. Specificity drives accuracy.
Don'tSwitch modules mid-conversation expecting continuityEach module has its own chat context. Switching resets the conversation — that's by design, not a bug.

A note on the Atelier lock

If you see a blurred section at the bottom of the app labeled "Generate Blueprint from Principles" — that's the Atelier-tier feature for turning your principle choices into a full WGS genome string and QACS placement coordinates. It's visible so you can see what's coming. Upgrade to Atelier from your account settings to unlock it.
