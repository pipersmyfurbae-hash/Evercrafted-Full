Three placement systems

The algorithm
shapes the design.

01
Golden Angle Spiral
137.5° increments
The same mathematical principle found in sunflowers and pinecones. Each element is placed 137.5° from the previous — creating maximum distribution without repetition. The result looks organic, lush, and effortlessly balanced.
Best for: naturalistic designs
02
Uniform Radial
Equal angular spacing
Elements distributed at perfectly equal angles around each ring. Creates a formal, symmetrical geometry — ideal for traditional or architectural wreaths where order communicates intentionality.
Best for: formal, symmetrical
03
Cluster Grouping
Cardinal + diagonal points
Elements grouped into three-stem clusters at the cardinal and diagonal positions. Mimics traditional hand-crafted wreath style — each cluster becomes a small composition within the larger design.
Best for: traditional, handcrafted
