How to use
WreathStudio

Everything you need to go from wreath blueprint to Midjourney-ready prompt in under 60 seconds — and keep going from there.

What WreathStudio does

WreathStudio converts your wreath design parameters into precision Midjourney prompts. Instead of writing prompts from scratch, you configure your design through structured inputs — and the engine assembles the right prompt language for your chosen output type.

There are two things WreathStudio is not:

⚠️
WreathStudio does not generate images.
It generates the prompt text you paste into Midjourney. You still use Midjourney to create the actual image.
✦
What it replaces
The 20–40 minutes you currently spend manually writing Midjourney prompts from scratch — figuring out what parameters to use, how to describe your florals, which lighting direction to specify.
✦ ✦ ✦

Your first prompt

From login to copied prompt in five steps.

1

Fill in Blueprint structure

Select your wreath's Shape and Season using the chip selectors. Then type your Focal flowers, Greenery, and Ribbon into the text fields. Be specific — ivory garden roses produces better results than roses.

2

Set emotion and target

Choose the Emotional mood that matches your design intent. This automatically maps to the right lighting descriptor in your prompt. Then pick your Prompt target — what kind of shot you want.

3

Pick your door color and aspect ratio

For hero and lifestyle shots, select the Door / wall color that contrasts your wreath. Choose an Aspect ratio — 2:3 is best for Etsy listings, 1:1 for Instagram.

4

Click "Generate prompt"

The prompt assembles instantly and appears in the output panel below. The parameter tags — --ar, --s, --chaos, --sw — are shown for quick reference.

5

Copy and paste into Midjourney

Click Copy to clipboard. Open Midjourney, type /imagine, paste your prompt, and press Enter. Before submitting, replace {your_lighting_ref_url} with your actual --sref URL — or remove the --sref parameter entirely for your first test.

Specificity tip
Include color modifiers and texture descriptors. soft dusty blue hydrangeas outperforms hydrangeas. For ribbon, include width, material, and drape: wide ivory linen ribbon with cascading tails.

Midjourney controls

Four sliders control how Midjourney renders your prompt. Defaults are optimized for product photography.

SliderRangeDefaultWhat it does
Blueprint fidelity0–10075Controls how strictly the prompt language tells Midjourney to follow your exact materials. Above 70 = "must follow exact details". 40–70 = allow tasteful variations. Below 40 = creative interpretation.
--stylize (--s)50–750180How strongly Midjourney applies its aesthetic training. Lower = closer to photo-real. Higher = more stylized, artistic, magazine-like. 100–200 is the sweet spot for product photography.
--chaos0–505How much variation between renders. 0–10 = predictable, consistent results. 20–50 = more unexpected outputs. Keep low (0–10) for shop hero images.
--sw (style weight)0–300100How much your --sref lighting reference influences the output. 60–120 = balanced. Above 200 = the reference dominates over your prompt. Only matters if you're using --sref.

Fidelity control

The fidelity slider changes the instruction language in your prompt — not a Midjourney parameter.

Fidelity 75–100:
"…must follow these exact material and structural details…"

Fidelity 40–74:
"…allow tasteful variations in secondary blooms and foliage…"

Fidelity 0–39:
"…allow creative interpretation inspired by the color story and emotion…"
✦
When to lower fidelity
If Midjourney isn't recognizing your specific florals (e.g. a proprietary name or niche variety), lower fidelity to 30–50. This lets Midjourney interpret the emotional and color intent rather than fighting to render an exact botanical it doesn't recognize.

Remix variants

Remix generates a slight compositional reinterpretation of your current prompt without changing any of your inputs.

It adds the phrase slight compositional reinterpretation to the base prompt, which signals Midjourney to vary the arrangement while keeping materials and mood consistent. Use it to get 3–4 different crops or angles from the same design brief.

⚠️
Craft plan and above
Remix is available on Craft, Studio, and Atelier tiers. Bloom users can upgrade to access it.

AI enhance

The AI Enhance button sends your generated prompt to Claude, which rewrites and enriches it with more specific lighting direction, surface detail, and atmosphere language — without changing your Midjourney parameters.

1

Generate your prompt first

AI Enhance works on an existing generated prompt. Click "Generate prompt" before hitting Enhance.

2

Click "✦ AI enhance"

Claude receives your current prompt and returns an enriched version. All --parameters are preserved unchanged.

3

Review and copy

The output panel updates with the enhanced prompt. If you prefer the original, click "Remix variant" to regenerate from your inputs.

Prompt history

Save any generated prompt to your history. Prompts are labeled with the target type and season for easy recall.

Click any saved prompt in the history panel to load it back into the output area. Bloom users can save 5 prompts. Craft and above have unlimited history.

Style references (--sref)

The --sref parameter tells Midjourney to match the lighting style of a reference image. WreathStudio includes a placeholder in every non-experimental prompt.

✦
How to use --sref
Find a photo with the lighting you love (warm moody, bright studio, golden hour). Upload it to any public image host (Imgur, Discord, your own CDN). Copy the direct image URL and replace {your_lighting_ref_url} in your copied prompt. The --sw value controls how dominant the reference is.

If you don't have a reference, simply delete --sref {your_lighting_ref_url} --sw 100 from your prompt before submitting to Midjourney. The prompt will still work — it just won't have a lighting reference applied.

FAQ

My output doesn't look like my wreath. What's wrong?
Usually a fidelity issue. Raise fidelity to 80–100 and be more specific in your focal flower field. Also check that your floral names are common enough for Midjourney to recognize — very niche botanical names may need to be simplified.
What's the best aspect ratio for Etsy listings?
2:3 (portrait) works best for Etsy hero images. It fills the listing card on both desktop and mobile. Use 1:1 for any secondary images you want to display in a grid.
Can I use WreathStudio for garlands or centerpieces?
Yes, with manual edits. Generate a prompt with the closest wreath shape, then manually edit the output to change "wreath" to "garland" or "centerpiece" before pasting into Midjourney. A dedicated garland/centerpiece mode is on the roadmap.
Why does my prompt still say {your_lighting_ref_url}?
That's a placeholder for your personal --sref URL. Either replace it with a real image URL before submitting to Midjourney, or delete the entire --sref {url} --sw 100 section from your prompt. Both work.
What does --style raw do?
--style raw tells Midjourney to render your prompt more literally, with less of its own "art direction" applied. This is important for product photography — without it, Midjourney tends to add painterly effects and over-stylized lighting that makes wreaths look less like real products.
How many prompts does each plan include?
Bloom (free): 25/month. Craft ($19/mo): 300/month. Studio ($49/mo): unlimited. Prompt counts reset on your billing date.
