1 Stage one

Seven questions.
Two minutes.

No forms, no color pickers, no style quizzes. A short guided conversation — one question at a time — gathers everything needed to write the story of this wreath. It takes about two minutes and feels nothing like filling out a brief.

What season does this memory live in? What time of day? Who was there? One sensory detail. What did it leave you with? Each question unlocks a layer that shapes the design.
The language never needs to be floral. "The smell of cedar and cold air and her" is a more complete design brief than any color palette could be.
Client commissions work exactly the same way. Take their words directly. What they tell you becomes what you build.

2 Stage two

Every piece
earns its place.

Grief reaches differently than joy. Peace holds still while longing moves. Evercrafted reads what you wrote and translates it into real choices — what shapes go in, how they sit, how they feel — before anything is placed.

The poem is not decorative. It is where the design begins. Once written, Evercrafted reads it the way a maker would — looking for what the writing carries, not just what was described.
A memory of peace often carries longing inside it. A memory of joy is often threaded with grief for what has passed. The design is made from all of it — not just what was named. The finishes, the accent pieces, the quieter moments between the blooms.
Every floral chosen traces back to something in the poem. What holds the design together. What gives it its voice. What you notice last when you look closely at the finished piece.
The location is understood, not guessed. Alfama. Amsterdam. Clintonville. Each place has a specific architecture, a specific light, a specific surface where a wreath genuinely belongs. Evercrafted finds it.

3 Stage three

Your materials
learn their language

Every piece in your collection gets understood. Drop a photo, paste a product name, or describe it yourself. Evercrafted reads what it sees and figures out where that piece belongs — what it brings to a design, and what kind of feeling it carries.

Every piece gets described in terms the design engine understands — what job it does, how it moves, how it looks in the light, and what feeling it carries.
Not everything is meant to lead. Some pieces are the ones the eye finds first. Others fill in quietly around them. Evercrafted knows which is which.
Three ways to tag: drop a photo, paste a product name from any supplier, or fill a guided form by hand. All three produce the same result.
Every suggestion is transparent. Where the system is certain, it shows you. Where it's less sure, it tells you that too — so you always make the final call.

4 Stage four

Designed to
surprise you.

Every blueprint pulls from a different corner of your collection. The same memory, explored twice, produces two genuinely different designs — because Evercrafted is built to resist the comfortable and the predictable.

The foundation layer builds from the emotional core of the memory. Each piece chosen here is what the eye finds first — the weight and shape of the whole design.
The detail layer fills in around the foundation. The quieter pieces, the textures, the small accents — chosen to sit beside the main blooms, not fight with them.
The result: every design is genuinely yours. No two designs land on the same combinations — even when they start from the same feeling. The comfortable and predictable are never the answer.

5 Stage five

Every piece finds
where it belongs

Composition is not about filling space. It is about deciding what the eye finds, and when. Evercrafted maps each chosen piece to the part of the wreath where it will do its best work, leaving the rest of the form deliberately open.

Every design has a place where things happen and a place where they do not. The open side of the wreath is just as intentional as the blooms — bare grapevine, breathing room, space that makes the rest feel richer.
Each piece has a natural home on the wreath. The heaviest, most important blooms sit closer to the center. Supporting pieces fill around them. Greenery spreads toward the edges and softens the whole shape.
Quantities are never guessed. A large bloom takes up more space than a delicate wisp — so it gets fewer stems. A fine, airy piece gets more. The counts that come out are the counts that actually build the wreath.
The AI reads the feeling. Evercrafted handles the design. Two separate jobs, done separately — so neither one compromises the other.

6 Stage six

Five outputs.
All at once.

Every memory becomes five things simultaneously — the poetic scene, the location map, the blueprint, the location-aware render prompt, and the build guide. Everything you and your client need, generated in one pass.

The poetic scene — 150 words that inhabit the memory. Written to be read, printed on a card, or shared with a client before the piece is built.
The location map — the place pinned. The architecture and lifestyle researched. The render setting chosen from what makes that specific place itself.
The render prompt — places the wreath in the actual world of the memory. Not a studio wall. Not a generic surface. The exact place where the design belongs, chosen from the location itself.
The blueprint and build guide — exact stem counts per zone, the polar placement map, and a sequenced construction guide. From screen to finished wreath without a gap.
