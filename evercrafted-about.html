The maker behind the platform

25 years making.
Still losing time to deciding.
Not anymore.

My name is Bret. I have been designing and building custom faux botanical wreaths for clients in Columbus, Ohio for over 25 years. I love the actual work — the layering, the balance, the moment a piece finally feels right.

What I did not love was how long it took to get there. Not the building. The deciding. Every commission started the same way — a client with a feeling they could not quite describe, and me trying to translate that into a wreath while staring at my inventory wondering where to begin.

Evercrafted is what I built when I got tired of that gap. It is a design tool that starts where the feeling starts — and handles everything between the memory and the build.

The real problem

Every wreath maker faces
the same invisible wall

You know how to build. After years in this craft — maybe a season, maybe a decade, maybe more — you have the materials, the taste, and the instincts. None of that is the problem.

The problem is the time between a client saying "I want something that feels like my grandmother" and the moment you actually pick up the first stem. That gap — where you are scrolling Pinterest, staring at your inventory, second-guessing every combination — is where the hours go.

It is not a skill problem. It is a translation problem. There was no system for moving from a feeling to a decision. Not after one year of making. Not after twenty-five. The gap between what a client describes and the moment you reach for the first stem does not close on its own — it just gets more familiar.

After 25 years of this work — hundreds of commissions, thousands of design decisions — I had enough of making those decisions slowly. I built a system that could do the translation for me. One that understood what a feeling looked like in physical materials and could tell me exactly what to reach for and why.

That system became Evercrafted.

Design philosophy

What I believe about
wreath design

These are the principles I built into Evercrafted — because a tool should reflect the way good designers actually think.

Feeling before form
A great wreath starts with understanding what it is meant to say. The materials come after. If you reach for the flowers before you know the feeling, you are guessing — and it shows.
Asymmetry is the point
Nature is not symmetrical. The most compelling wreaths are not either. Tension, movement, and intentional negative space are what make a piece look alive instead of assembled.
The silence matters
Negative space is not empty — it is breathing room. A wreath that fills every inch gives the eye nowhere to rest. What you leave out is as important as what you put in.
Repetition is the enemy
Every design should bring something the last one did not. The moment your work starts to look familiar — even to you — it is time to change the way you are making decisions.
Inventory should work for you
Your collection of materials is not a storage problem. It is a design resource. The more you understand what each piece brings to a design, the faster and better your decisions get.
Build what you decide
The hours are in the deciding, not the building. Get the design right on paper — or on screen — and the build becomes the enjoyable part it was always meant to be.
How we got here

From one maker's
frustration to a platform

25 years ago
The first custom commissions
Started designing and building custom faux botanical wreaths in Columbus — a craft that became a career, and a career that became a calling. Even then, every client had a feeling they wanted the piece to hold, and turning that feeling into a design took far longer than the build itself.
Over two decades
The pattern became clear
Every commission followed the same arc. A conversation full of feeling and meaning, then a design process that felt like starting over every time. The building was fast. The deciding was slow. That ratio was wrong.
The turning point
Building a system that could translate
Started building what would become Evercrafted — a way to move from a client's words directly into design decisions. Not a mood board, not a color picker. A real tool that understood emotion as a design input.
Now
Opening it up to other makers
Evercrafted is the tool I built for my own practice — now being shaped into something other wreath artists can use. The waitlist is open. The platform is in development. The first makers get in before the public launch, with founding pricing that stays with them.
